Alaska Highway fuel strategy — fill at every townFort St. John (Mile 47) is the last city. After that: Pink Mountain (Mile 143), Toad River (Mile 422). Never pass a fuel station with less than half a tank on the Alcan. The GX470's ~400 mi range is comfortable but don't test it.

A
7:30 AMDEPART
Dawson Creek — Alaska Highway, Mile 0
You're on the Alcan now. The road north of Dawson Creek is good paved highway for the first 50 miles — two lanes, modest traffic. The landscape quickly transitions to boreal forest and rolling terrain. First wildlife sightings common here: black bears, deer, the occasional moose.

Top off here without fail. Fort St. John is a real city with Costco, supermarkets, and cheap fuel. After this, services become sparse and expensive. Stock up on any last groceries, snacks, or supplies you need for the next few days.
↳ Last Costco fuel for a long stretch north

Northern Rockies — most remote section of the AlcanMuncho Lake to Liard Hot Springs has no services for ~100 km. Fill at every stop. June brings long daylight (18+ hrs this far north) but also fresh snowfall at elevation. Road can have frost heaves and wildlife in road — moose, bison, bears. Speed accordingly.
WAYPOINTS
A
7:00 AMDEPART
Fort Nelson, BC — Northern Rockies entry
North of Fort Nelson, the Alaska Highway enters the Northern Rockies Mountains Provincial Park. The terrain becomes dramatically more rugged — the road follows river valleys hemmed in by serrated peaks. Stone Mountain Provincial Park begins at Mile 373.
B
8:30 AM · mi 80FUEL
Toad River Lodge — Mile 422
Small roadside lodge with fuel, basic food, and a famous hat collection (covering the ceiling). Fill up without question — this is one of only two fuel stops in 530 miles. The lodge is a genuine Alaska Highway institution.
↳ Fill completely — next fuel: Muncho Lake ~50 mi
C
10:00 AM · mi 150POI
Muncho Lake Provincial Park — Mile 456
The jade-green color of Muncho Lake comes from copper oxide leaching from surrounding rock. The highway hugs the eastern shore for several kilometers — one of the iconic Alaska Highway photographs. Stone sheep on the cliff faces above the road. The lake is ~11 km long and strikingly beautiful.
↳ PHOTO: Pull-out at the north end of lake — best angle
D
11:00 AM · mi 195POI
Liard Hot Springs Provincial Park — Mile 497
Do not skip this. The Alpha Pool is a natural hot spring at 42°C surrounded by a boreal jungle — orchids and tropical-feeling vegetation heated by the geothermal water. A 500m boardwalk leads to the pool. Bears are commonly seen here. One of the great unexpected wonders of the Alaska Highway.
↳ Budget 1.5 hrs — worth every minute. Watch for bears on boardwalk.
E
12:30 PM · mi 255BORDER
BC / Yukon Territory Border — Mile 588
No formal border crossing — just a sign. Entering the Yukon Territory. Speed limits change (in km/h). The landscape opens dramatically — wide river valleys, massive boreal plateaus, and the sense of genuine remoteness that defines the Yukon. Welcome to real north.
↳ PHOTO: BC/YT sign — expedition milestone
Z
~1:30 PM · mi 330CAMP
Watson Lake, YT — Watson Lake Campground
Watson Lake is famous for the Sign Post Forest — started in 1942 by a homesick soldier who posted a sign pointing to his hometown. Now has 100,000+ signs. Add your own. Town has fuel, a grocery store, and a good diner. Afternoon arrival means time to walk the forest and explore.
↳ BRING A SIGN: print your hometown/street name before leaving

Whitehorse — Yukon capital and major resupply hubWhitehorse is the largest city between Edmonton and Anchorage. Use the afternoon well — full vehicle service, laundry, quality restaurant meal, resupply everything. This is the last real city before the Alcan enters Alaska.
WAYPOINTS
A
8:00 AMDEPART
Watson Lake, YT
North on the Alaska Highway through boreal Yukon. The road is excellent — smooth, wide, good sight lines. Wildlife sightings increase: caribou, moose, wolves. The scale of the landscape becomes almost abstract — rolling tundra and boreal forest to every horizon.
B
9:30 AM · mi 90FOOD
Teslin, YT — Nisutlin Trading Post
Teslin is a small First Nations community on Teslin Lake — one of the longest lakes in the Yukon. Good quick stop for coffee and a bite. The George Johnston Museum here covers Tlingit history and Alaska Highway construction from a First Nations perspective. Worth 30 minutes.
C
10:30 AM · mi 155SCENIC
Jake's Corner — Tagish Road junction
If time permits and weather is good, the detour south to Carcross on the Tagish Road (~45 min) passes through some of the most stunning lake-and-mountain scenery in the Yukon. Carcross Desert is the world's smallest desert — a sand dune system between two lakes. Otherwise continue straight to Whitehorse.
↳ Optional: Carcross detour adds ~1 hr but is exceptional
D
12:30 PM · mi 270FOOD
Whitehorse — Burnt Toast Café
One of Whitehorse's best lunch spots — generous sandwiches, excellent soups, good coffee. Whitehorse's food scene is surprisingly strong for a city of 28,000. Other great options: Antoinette's (Caribbean), The Wheelhouse (pub), Sanchez Cantina (Mexican).
↳ ORDER: club sandwich, house soup, yukon gold coffee
E
1:30 PM · mi 270FUEL
Whitehorse — Costco fuel
Whitehorse has a Costco — the cheapest fuel in the Yukon. Fill completely. Next leg takes you toward the Alaska border where fuel is priced at a premium. Whitehorse is also where you should service the vehicle if anything has felt off in the first 8 days.
↳ Last Costco pricing until Fairbanks, AK
Z
~2:00 PM · mi 270CAMP
Whitehorse — Hi Country RV Park
Well-run RV park close to the Yukon River. Full hookups, hot showers, laundry. Use the evening to walk the Whitehorse riverfront trail — the SS Klondike sternwheeler is a National Historic Site worth seeing. Good energy in town in June with the long daylight.
